What Peregrino Sol Is

Architecture
that listens
to nature.

An architectural experience on 15 private acres in Paris, Texas. Designed by Mexico City's oioioi Studio — winner of the Architectural Digest Mexico Design Award. Featured in Dwell Magazine.

Peregrino Sol is the first O-Frame cabin in the United States. The signature feature: a 4.5-foot circular porthole window — positioned so that when you wake up, the first thing you see is sunrise, framed like a living painting.

Curved roofline. Dark-stained wood. A semicircular skylight overhead so you fall asleep under stars. Inside: everything intentional, nothing wasted. Outside: open meadow, a private hot tub, fire pit, 15 acres of silence all your own. A world away from everything else.

This isn't accommodation. It's architecture you can sleep in.

The Design

Curves instead
of corners.

oioioi Studio had a clear mandate: no A-frames, no generic wood cabins, no walls of glass that impose on the landscape they're meant to frame.

"Instead of these A-frames or container-like cabins, we thought about how we could break the box — or change the experience of the window, or how you could enter."

Ernesto Pérez Rea Juncá · oioioi Studio

The O-Frame is the answer. A curved silhouette with no corners anywhere on the structure. A circular window placed by the architects themselves — sized and positioned so when you open your eyes in the morning, you're looking at sunrise through a perfect circle of glass. A rounded wall that slides fully open so you can lie in bed and feel the outside air.

"You get rid of what's not necessary, and you get the simplest way possible."

Pablo Germenos Garcia · oioioi Studio

Now it's here — the first O-Frame cabin in the United States — on 15 private acres in Paris, Texas. Ninety minutes from Dallas. Completely your own.
